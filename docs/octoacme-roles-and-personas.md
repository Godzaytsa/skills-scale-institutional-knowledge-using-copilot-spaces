# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing

### Role Summary
QA and Testing professionals ensure that software meets acceptance criteria, quality standards, and user expectations. They validate features, identify defects, and advocate for quality throughout the delivery process.

### Responsibilities
- Define and execute test plans aligned to acceptance criteria
- Perform manual and automated testing (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Identify, document, and triage defects with clear reproduction steps
- Validate fixes and sign off on feature readiness
- Collaborate with Developers on test coverage and testability improvements
- Monitor quality metrics and trends

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Catch defects early and reduce production incidents
- Provide clear feedback to improve product quality

### Typical Communication
- QA test planning and review meetings
- Defect triage and closure discussions
- Sign-off on feature acceptance
- Quality metrics and trend reports

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, document business goals, and facilitate translation of stakeholder needs into actionable development plans. They bridge communication between stakeholders, Product Managers, and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and elicit requirements
- Document business goals, constraints, and success criteria
- Draft and refine acceptance criteria with Product Managers and Developers
- Identify dependencies and integration points
- Assist with risk identification and mitigation planning
- Validate solutions against stated business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business objectives and technical solutions
- Minimize scope drift and miscommunication
- Enable clear, testable acceptance criteria

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering sessions
- Backlog refinement and acceptance criteria workshops
- Design reviews and solution validation meetings
- Documentation and requirements traceability

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers focus on usability, accessibility, and aesthetics to maximize user satisfaction and engagement. They collaborate with Product Managers to understand user needs and provide implementation guidance to Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and design specifications
- Ensure designs are accessible and follow UX best practices
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user flows and interaction patterns
- Support Developers with design specs and component requirements
- Participate in design reviews and validate implementation

### Goals
- Deliver user-friendly and accessible solutions
- Validate design decisions through user feedback
- Reduce design ambiguity and rework during development

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and feedback sessions
- Usability testing and user research findings
- Design specs and component documentation
- Demo sessions and implementation validation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers ensure smooth deployments, coordinate release timing, and communicate status and risks to stakeholders. They own the release process and minimize risk during deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Own release process and deployment schedule
- Coordinate with Engineering, QA, and stakeholders on release readiness
- Develop and maintain rollback and mitigation plans
- Prepare release notes and deployment runbooks
- Communicate release status and timing to stakeholders
- Facilitate post-deployment verification and incident response

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, predictable releases
- Minimize risk and downtime during deployments
- Maintain stakeholder confidence through clear communication

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and coordination meetings
- Stakeholder updates and announcement communications
- Incident response and post-deployment verification calls
- Release notes and deployment documentation

---

## Support Specialist

### Role Summary
Support Specialists monitor production health, respond to user and customer issues, and capture feedback to inform future improvements. They serve as a critical link between customers and the product team.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor production systems for issues and anomalies
- Triage and respond to customer/user support requests
- Escalate production incidents to Engineering with clear context
- Capture and communicate user feedback and feature requests
- Assist in root cause analysis for critical issues
- Contribute to post-release health assessments and retrospectives

### Goals
- Enhance service reliability and customer satisfaction
- Enable rapid incident response and resolution
- Provide actionable customer insights to drive product improvements

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket triage and escalation
- Incident response and status updates
- Product feedback and feature request summaries
- Post-incident reviews and health reports
